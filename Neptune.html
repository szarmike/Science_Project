Neptune

Name Origin: Neptune is named after the Roman god of the sea.

Discovered by: Johann Galle & Urbain Le Verrier in 1846.

Average distance from Earth: 2.7 billion miles

Things to know:

  • One day is 16h 6m.
  • One year is 164.8 Earth years.
  • Gravity is 114% of Earth's.
  • Has 14 known moons.

Atmosphere:

H₂: 80%

He: 19%

CH₄: 1.5%

Interesting Facts:

  • Neptune has the fastest wind speeds in the solar system, reaching up to 1,200 mph.
  • The Great Dark Spot on Neptune can appear and vanish over time, unlike Jupiter’s permanent storm.
