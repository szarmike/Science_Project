Saturn

Name Origin: Saturn is named after the Roman god of agriculture.

Known since: Observed by ancient astronomers.

Average distance from Earth: 886 million miles

Things to know:

  • One day is 10h 33m.
  • One year is 29.46 Earth years.
  • Gravity is 107% of Earth's.
  • Has 82 known moons.

Atmosphere:

H₂: 96.3%

He: 3.25%

Interesting Facts:

  • Saturn’s north pole hosts a hexagon-shaped storm over 20,000 miles wide.
  • Some of Saturn’s rings are as thin as 10 meters, despite spanning hundreds of thousands of kilometers.
