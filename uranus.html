Uranus

Name Origin: Uranus is named after the Greek god of the sky, Ouranos.

Discovered by: William Herschel on March 13, 1781.

Average distance from Earth: 1.784 billion miles

Things to know:

  • One day is 17h 14m.
  • One year is 84 Earth years.
  • Gravity is 86% of Earth's.
  • Has 28 moons.

Atmosphere:

H₂: 82.5%

He: 15.2%

CH₄: 2.3%

Interesting Facts:

  • Uranus’s magnetic field is tilted and offset from its center by about one-third of the planet's radius.
  • Uranus emits almost no internal heat, unlike other giant planets.
